Predicting Road Accident Severity in the UK

A full predictive analytics and machine learning pipeline on 307,973 UK police-reported road accidents, comparing CART, Random Forest, and Logistic Regression to classify accident severity as Slight, Serious, or Fatal.

Business Problem

Road accidents cost lives, money, and emergency resources. If we can understand when and where severe accidents are most likely to happen, authorities can allocate resources more effectively and prevent fatalities.

Core question: Can we predict whether a UK road accident will be Slight, Serious, or Fatal based on known conditions at the time of the incident?

Key challenge: The severe and fatal classes are rare (class imbalance), meaning accuracy alone is a misleading metric. Precision, Recall, and F1-score are the true performance indicators - and the data requires balancing before operational deployment.

Data Preparation

Source: UK police-reported road accidents dataset, 307,973 records. Features include road and lighting conditions, weather, location (lat/long), number of vehicles and casualties, time and day, and urban vs rural area.

Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA revealed that severity is heavily skewed toward Slight accidents, most incidents occur on weekdays, and location and time are stronger predictors than weather or road surface.

Severe Class Imbalance86% of accidents are Slight, ~12.6% are Serious, and only 1.4% are Fatal. This extreme imbalance means accuracy alone is misleading - the model must be evaluated on precision, recall, and F1.
Weekday Concentration - Peak on FridayMost accidents occur on weekdays with Friday being the busiest day. Sunday has the lowest count, confirming that commuter traffic patterns drive accident frequency.
Location and Time are Key CorrelatesLatitude, Longitude, and Time show the strongest correlations with severity. Weather and road surface show weaker relationships than expected - a counterintuitive but important finding.
Longitude, Latitude, and Time LeadThe Random Forest confirms that geographic location (Longitude 0.159, Latitude 0.157) and time (0.155) are the top predictors. Local Authority District and Accident Month follow, while Weather ranks much lower.

Three classifiers were trained and compared on accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. All models used the same 17 engineered features and 80/20 stratified split.

  • All models achieve ~85% accuracy - but this is misleading due to class imbalance. A model predicting "Slight" every time would also score ~86%.
  • Random Forest has the strongest balanced performance - highest precision (0.376) meaning it makes fewer false positive severe predictions.
  • Logistic Regression struggles with non-linear relationships - lowest precision despite marginally higher accuracy.
  • CART is stable but less consistent than the ensemble Random Forest across data subsets.
Feature Importance finding: The model revealed that location (Longitude 0.159, Latitude 0.157) and time (0.155) are the strongest predictors of severity - not weather or road surface as commonly assumed. This is a counterintuitive and actionable finding for road safety planning.

Key Findings & Recommendations

Target High-Risk Locations

Since location (lat/long and district) is the top predictor, authorities should map high-severity accident zones and concentrate infrastructure improvements and speed controls in those specific areas.

Strengthen Lighting and Speed Controls

In areas with historically high serious-accident rates, improve street lighting and enforce speed limits more strictly - both features the model identifies as having moderate predictive power.

Seasonal Safety Campaigns

Accident Month is the 5th most important feature. Deploy targeted road safety campaigns and increased enforcement during peak accident months identified by the model.

Real-Time Prediction Dashboard

Deploy Random Forest as a decision-support tool in dispatch systems. When an incident is reported, the model can instantly estimate likely severity to help route the appropriate emergency response.

Future enhancements:

  • Apply SMOTE oversampling to improve Fatal and Serious class recall
  • Try advanced models like XGBoost or LightGBM
  • Build an interactive geospatial heatmap dashboard
  • Add socioeconomic and road infrastructure features for richer prediction
